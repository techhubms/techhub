# How GitHub Copilot and Andela Are Expanding AI Skills Globally

Developers worldwide possess immense talent, but access to cutting-edge technologies and mentorship has historically been uneven. Recognizing this, GitHub and Andela have collaborated over the past two years to expand structured AI access throughout Andela's 5.5-million-member developer network.

## Real-World AI Training with GitHub Copilot

In 2024, Andela rolled out structured AI training focused on developers whose work involves complex production systems, particularly in Africa and Latin America. Instead of treating AI as a detached experiment, Copilot was integrated into genuine development workflows—within IDEs, code reviews, and live code refactoring. Developers were selected for training based on their roles and responsibilities, ensuring practical relevance.

> *"Training has to reflect what developers are actually asked to do at work, not idealized exercises."*  
> — Abraham Omomoh, Learning Program Manager at Andela

## Addressing Global Developer Challenges

Developers in regions like Africa, South America, and Southeast Asia face obstacles such as unreliable internet, costly cloud tools, and limited compute resources. Generic, non-localized training content further complicates their upskilling.

Structured, affordable access to AI tools and community-driven learning ecosystems is essential to avoid widening existing inequities.

## Integrating AI Into Production Systems

For most working developers, experimental side-projects are unrealistic—their learning must take place within their actual jobs. Andela’s program embeds Copilot usage into the maintenance and development of real systems, with training tailored to production constraints rather than hypothetical scenarios.

Developers reported using Copilot for:

- Generating unit test coverage for legacy codebases
- Drafting safe refactorings and clarifying control flow
- Sketching architecture diagrams

> *"Legacy code usually doesn’t have coverage. So I use [Copilot] to build that coverage first. Then I know what I’m playing with."*  
> — Daniel Nascimento, Senior Engineer

## Productivity Gains and Professional Growth

Developers noticed:

- Faster orientation in unfamiliar systems
- Greater confidence and ownership of complex projects
- Less repetitive setup, more focus on high-impact work

Daniel Nascimento estimated a roughly 50% productivity boost, emphasizing time saved on repetitive tasks.

Structured Copilot exposure led to expanded professional skills, increased credibility, and more ambitious roles for participating engineers.

## The Real AI Skills Gap: Access, Not Ability

The article argues the true AI gap is access to tools, mentorship, and safe experimentation. Where structured access exists, developer growth compounds. Importantly, expanded access in the Global South is not about “catching up,” but about ensuring diverse global voices are building the future of AI.

Koffi Kelvin, an Andela engineer, notes:

> *"GitHub Copilot is a portal that catapulted my professional trajectory into a literal other dimension."*

## Conclusion: Compounding Benefits Through Structured Access

AI upskilling is most effective when integrated with real work. Intentional, well-supported access to technologies like GitHub Copilot allows developers worldwide to contribute confidently to modern, global engineering teams.
