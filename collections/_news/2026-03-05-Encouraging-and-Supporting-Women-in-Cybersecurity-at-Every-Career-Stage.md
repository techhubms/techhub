# Encouraging and Supporting Women in Cybersecurity at Every Career Stage

*By Vasu Jakkal*

Women’s History Month and International Women’s Day are key opportunities to reflect on the progress and contributions of women in cybersecurity. While highlighting the incredible achievements of women leaders, the article draws attention to women early in their cybersecurity journeys—those building skills and resilience in an evolving landscape defined by rapid advances in AI and growing threats.

## The Importance of Diversity in Cybersecurity

Diversity in perspective is presented as a non-negotiable aspect of effective cybersecurity. The article cites research from ISACA showing that homogenous teams are less effective in threat modeling and innovation. Microsoft Security stresses that reflecting the complexity of modern threats requires equally broad and inclusive security teams.

### Key Points

- Diverse teams ask better questions, spot risks sooner, and create more robust, inclusive security.
- Women bring unique qualities—such as empathy, systems thinking, and collaborative leadership—that are key to security and AI advancement.

## Supporting Women at Every Career Stage

The article offers actionable recommendations for widening and strengthening the pipeline of women in cybersecurity, including:

1. **Share diverse role models:** Representation inspires early-career women and guides those at higher levels to stay and lead.
2. **Reevaluate job descriptions:** Avoiding narrow definitions ensures qualified candidates aren’t deterred from joining or advancing.
3. **Continuous education and training:** Early, accessible training should be matched by ongoing upskilling and leadership support.
4. **Volunteer and advocate:** Engagement with organizations like Girl Security and WiCyS provides opportunities for growth and mentorship.
5. **Mentorship and sponsorship:** Formal networks and advocacy are among the most powerful drivers of career progression.
6. **Daily allyship:** Creating space and offering advocacy are crucial for retention and advancement.

## Microsoft’s Commitment

Microsoft’s mission to empower every person includes fostering a cybersecurity and AI security community that is equitable and accessible. The article encourages readers to celebrate not only established women leaders, but also women entering and advancing through the field now. Security is presented as a team effort that benefits from the full spectrum of talent and perspectives.
