# Building sovereign AI at the edge: Microsoft and Armada collaborate to deliver Azure Local on Galleon modular datacenters

Explore how Microsoft and Armada bring sovereign AI to the edge with Azure Local, enabling secure, resilient workloads in disconnected and regulated environments.

## Overview

As governments and regulated industries pursue digital transformation, a recurring requirement is running mission-critical workloads where data originates—while maintaining sovereignty, resilience, and operational control.

- [Build sovereign edge solutions with Azure Local](https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/products/local/)

Microsoft and Armada announced a collaboration to deliver **sovereign AI at the edge** by bringing **Microsoft Sovereign Private Cloud** capabilities to **Armada’s Galleon modular datacenters (MDC)**. The goal is to enable customer-controlled environments that can operate in:

- Intermittently connected locations
- Contested environments
- Fully disconnected scenarios

This is positioned as a way to deliver Azure’s operating model, security posture, and AI-ready capabilities where traditional public cloud approaches aren’t feasible.

![Sovereign Private Cloud architecture showing Azure Local deployed in Armada Galleon modular datacenters, with local control plane, Foundry Local and M365 Local workloads, supporting secure, disconnected operations without reliance on a public cloud region.](https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/blog/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Armada-Body-Final-1920-x-1080-1024x576.webp)

## Meeting sovereign requirements anywhere

The post calls out scenarios common to defense, public safety, energy, and critical infrastructure where public cloud use may not be possible. Requirements include:

- Disconnected or limited connectivity
- Portable or rapidly deployable infrastructure
- Strict data residency and regulatory controls
- Support for modern AI and analytics workloads at the edge

Microsoft and Armada describe delivering a **validated sovereign reference architecture** showing how **Sovereign Private Cloud** operates on and interoperates with the **Armada Edge Platform**, to deploy Azure services closer to where data is produced while keeping customer control of data, operations, and governance.

- [See how Microsoft supports digital sovereignty](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/sovereignty)

## Azure Local in Armada’s Galleon modular datacenters

The core technical building block is **Azure Local** (Microsoft’s on-premises cloud platform for disconnected and sovereign scenarios), combined with:

- **Armada’s Galleon MDC**
- **Armada Edge Platform (AEP)**

- [Azure Local](https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/products/local/)

The solution is described as supporting:

- An **Azure Local control plane** and **managed clusters**, including **multi-rack scalability**
- Flexible storage architectures, including:
  - **Hyperconverged** deployments
  - **SAN-backed** deployments
- Resilient multi-network connectivity spanning:
  - Satellite
  - LTE/5G
  - RF
  - SD-WAN
- Security, compliance, and hardening aligned to sovereign/government/regulatory workloads

The intended result is an edge platform deployable in remote, mobile, or constrained environments while still leveraging Azure’s consistent operating model.

![Armada Galleon modular datacenter units arranged in a row inside a dark industrial facility, with Armada branding visible on the central unit.](https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/blog/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Armada-image-1024x313.webp)

## Enabling sovereign AI and mission-critical workloads

The post frames this collaboration as going beyond infrastructure to deliver **sovereign AI capabilities at the edge**.

As part of Microsoft Sovereign Private Cloud, **Foundry Local** and **Azure Local** are positioned to let customers deploy, govern, and operate AI entirely within their trusted boundary—supporting national sovereignty, classified workloads, and highly regulated data pipelines.

- [Foundry Local: Get started](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/azure/foundry-local/get-started)

With **Foundry Local**, customers can run **AI inference and analytics locally**, including when disconnected from public cloud.

Benefits called out:

- Processing sensitive data locally to meet sovereignty requirements
- Reducing latency for real-time decision-making
- Operating AI workloads in austere or bandwidth-constrained environments

- [Explore how Azure Local powers sovereign edge deployments](https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/products/local/)

## A shared vision for sovereign edge infrastructure

> Customers operating in the world’s most demanding environments don’t have the luxury of choosing between sovereignty, resilience, and modern cloud capabilities, they need all three. By partnering with Microsoft, we’re combining Armada’s deployable, mission ready infrastructure with Azure Local’s consistent cloud platform to help governments and regulated industries run secure, AI enabled workloads anywhere they operate, even when connectivity is limited or unavailable. Together, Microsoft and Armada are delivering a practical path to sovereign AI at the edge, one that respects local control, supports disconnected operations, and scales from today’s mission critical needs to tomorrow’s intelligent systems.
> —*Dan Wright, Co-Founder and CEO of Armada*
