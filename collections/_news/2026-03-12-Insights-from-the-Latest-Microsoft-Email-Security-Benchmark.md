# Insights from the Latest Microsoft Email Security Benchmark

Microsoft's latest benchmarking report continues its tradition of transparency by publishing detailed performance data on email security. The findings are valuable for security teams evaluating both Microsoft Defender for Office 365 and partner solutions.

## What's New in the Benchmarking Data

- **Microsoft Defender removes an average of 70.8% of malicious email post-delivery**, reducing dwell time even for threats that pass initial filters.
- **Layered Protection**: Combining Microsoft Defender with Integrated Cloud Email Security (ICES) partners further increases detection rates, especially for promotional and spam email.
- **Overlapping Detections**: Both Defender and ICES solutions may flag the same items, with incremental improvement depending on scenario and message type.
- **Data-Driven Visibility**: Benchmarking offers security teams insights not only into *whether* threats are blocked, but *how* defenses contribute across the email attack lifecycle.

[Read the full benchmarking data](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/business/siem-and-xdr/microsoft-defender-office-365/performance-benchmarking)

## Key Results by Vendor Category

### ICES (Integrated Cloud Email Security) Vendors

- **Productivity Boosts**: Layering ICES with Defender cuts marketing and bulk email by an average of 13.7%, reducing inbox clutter.
- **Incremental Value**: Spam and malicious email filtering gains are more modest with ICES, averaging under 0.3% improvement compared to earlier periods.
- **Post-Delivery Remediation**: Defender's zero hour auto-purge handled most post-delivery threats (70.8%), while ICES vendors provided supplementary filtering (29.2%).

![ICES vendor contribution chart](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Picture1.webp)

### SEG (Secure Email Gateway) Vendors

- **Missed Cyberthreats**: Microsoft Defender outperformed SEG vendors in catching high-severity threats, missing fewer per 1,000 protected users than competitors.

![SEG missed threats chart](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Picture2.webp)

## Expanding the ICES Vendor Ecosystem

- Microsoft is prioritizing ecosystem integrations, currently partnering with Darktrace, KnowBe4, Cisco, and VIPRE Security Group for enhanced defense.
- Customers maintain a single operational plane within Defender, benefiting from combined detection and unified workflows.
- New integrations are aimed at enhancing clarity, defense-in-depth, and user experience without increasing complexity.

Read more on the [ICES vendor ecosystem](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/defender-office-365/mdo-ices-vendor-ecosystem).

## Why It Matters

- **Email Attacks Remain Prevalent**: As attackers evolve, layered and integrated defenses become essential.
- **Clear Insight**: Regular, transparent benchmarking empowers teams to make better security decisions.
- **Unified Operations**: Integration minimizes investigation friction, reducing time to respond and manage threats.
- **Data-Driven Decisions**: Visibility into both native and partner solution performance ensures organizations can optimize for their unique environment.

## Looking Ahead

Microsoft commits to ongoing benchmarking and investment in the Defender and ICES partner ecosystem to offer ever-improving, unified email security.
