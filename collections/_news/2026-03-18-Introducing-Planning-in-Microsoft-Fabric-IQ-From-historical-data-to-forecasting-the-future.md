# Introducing Planning in Microsoft Fabric IQ: From historical data to forecasting the future

*If you haven’t already, check out Arun Ulag’s hero blog “FabCon and SQLCon 2026: Unifying databases and Fabric on a single, complete platform” for a complete look at all of the FabCon and SQLCon announcements across both Fabric and Microsoft’s database offerings:* https://aka.ms/FabCon-SQLCon-2026-news

At **FabCon Atlanta**, Microsoft announced **Planning in Fabric IQ**: an enterprise planning capability designed to bring budgeting, forecasting, targets, and scenario planning into **Microsoft Fabric’s** governed data and semantic layer. The capability is co-engineered with **Lumel**.

The stated problem: analytics (dashboards, reports, KPIs) is common, but planning is often handled in separate tools that require manual reconciliation and data movement, leading to drift between plans and actuals.

## Planning in Fabric IQ

Traditional planning tools are described as sitting outside the data platform, which commonly results in:

- Plans drifting away from actuals
- Teams operating with different versions of the truth
- Forecasts lagging behind real-world change
- AI systems lacking business intent needed to act responsibly

The core idea of Planning in Fabric IQ is to connect **historical performance, current operations, and future intent** by placing planning on the same **governed, semantic foundation** as enterprise data.

## Planning capabilities

### Planning, natively built in Microsoft Fabric

Planning in Fabric IQ is positioned as natively integrated with Fabric components, including:

- **Power BI semantic models** (trusted business metrics)
- **Fabric SQL** (for writeback operations)
- **OneLake** (unified analytical and operational data)

Instead of exporting data into separate planning silos, teams can:

- Start from historical performance
- Add targets and forecasts
- Collaborate in-context
- Write projections back securely
- See impacts flow through connected reports and dashboards
- Use notifications for review/approval actions

Figure: Planning in Fabric IQ

The post also calls out governance and reduced integration overhead:

- “Without breaking governance, duplicating data, or introducing new integration overhead.”
- **OneLake** mirroring and shortcut capabilities are positioned as enabling planning workflows without needing ETL.

### Integrated budgeting, forecasting, and scenario modeling

Fabric Planning includes planning features such as:

- Budgeting
- Forecasting
- Scenario analysis
- What-if assessments
- Top-down and bottom-up workflows

### Collaborative enterprise planning

For multi-team planning, the announcement highlights:

- Workflow management
- Approval processes
- Audit trails
- Role-based access controls
- Governance features

### No-code self-service experience

Planning is aimed at business users with a **no-code** experience intended to reduce heavy IT involvement and shorten rollout time for planning apps.

### Seamless integration with analytics and reporting

The announcement claims integrated planning + analytics enables:

- Plan vs actuals analysis
- Interactive dashboards
- IBCS-compliant financial visualizations
- Ad hoc analysis

## From static plans to business context

The post argues that planning becomes part of the organization’s **business model** when it sits alongside operational/analytical data:

- Actuals, targets, and assumptions stay aligned
- Variance analysis becomes immediate
- Reference data updates flow through in real time
- Scenarios adjust dynamically as conditions change

## Planning as the foundation for intelligent action (AI intent layer)

The announcement positions planning as the “business intent layer” needed for **AI-assisted decision making**, where systems need not only data but also:

- Goals
- Targets
- Constraints
- Tradeoffs

It also references a direction toward **AI agents** that can reason over data, recommend actions, and operate within defined boundaries, grounded in the organization’s shared semantic model.
