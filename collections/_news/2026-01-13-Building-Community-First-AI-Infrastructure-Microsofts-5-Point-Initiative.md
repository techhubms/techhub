# Building Community-First AI Infrastructure: Microsoft’s 5-Point Initiative

Microsoft has launched a new 'Community-First AI Infrastructure' initiative in 2026 to redefine how large-scale AI infrastructure is built and operated in the United States. The initiative is anchored in five core commitments to the local communities where Microsoft designs, builds, and manages datacenters:

## 1. Paying for Datacenter Electricity Without Impacting Residents

- Microsoft commits to ensuring the costs of electricity used by its datacenters are fully paid by the company and not passed on to residential customers.
- Efforts include working with utilities and public commissions to set fair rates, collaborating on grid expansion, leveraging AI for increased efficiency, and advocating for supportive public policy.

## 2. Minimizing Water Use and Replenishing Community Water Supplies

- Advanced use of closed-loop and efficient cooling systems reduces water consumption in datacenters.
- Investments are made in local water infrastructure, water reuse projects, leak detection, and wetland restoration to replenish more water than is withdrawn.
- Microsoft publishes transparent water-use and replenishment data at the regional level, and advocates for sustainable water policies.

## 3. Creating Jobs and Workforce Training for Local Residents

- New datacenters create thousands of construction jobs and hundreds of operational roles.
- Microsoft partners with unions, apprenticeship programs, and community colleges (e.g., the Datacenter Academy program) to train local workers for careers in datacenter operations and skilled trades.
- The company also advocates for national apprenticeship policies and supports workforce development in underserved or rural regions.

## 4. Contributing to the Local Tax Base

- Microsoft pays its full share of local property taxes to support hospitals, schools, parks, and libraries — fueling local public services and amenities.
- The approach aims to ensure that datacenter communities see tangible economic and social benefits, citing examples of positive impact in Quincy, Washington, and other regions.

## 5. Investing in Local AI Training and Nonprofits

- The initiative delivers free, age-appropriate AI literacy and skills training in partnership with schools, community colleges, libraries, and chambers of commerce near datacenter regions.
- Small businesses, adults, and nonprofits all benefit from flexible grants, training hubs, and Microsoft employee volunteer support.
- Local liaisons are provided to coordinate community engagement and better align efforts with local needs.

---

## Conclusion

Drawing lessons from the history of US infrastructure expansion, Microsoft’s approach emphasizes the critical role of local communities and the responsibility of private companies to contribute positively. This initiative sets a precedent for responsible, transparent, and community-focused growth as AI technologies continue to reshape the global economy.
