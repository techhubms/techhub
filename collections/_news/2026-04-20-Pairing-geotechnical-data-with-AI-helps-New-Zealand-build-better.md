In this Microsoft news story, stclarke describes how New Zealand’s Geotechnical Database is hosted on Azure and queried in natural language via an AI assistant built with Azure OpenAI in Azure AI Foundry, helping engineers quickly find site investigation data for planning and construction decisions.<!--excerpt_end-->

# Pairing geotechnical data with AI helps New Zealand build better

Olivia Ellis-Garland, an engineering geologist, demonstrates a workflow where she clicks on a map of New Zealand and types a natural-language request:

> “Show me the investigations in Hobsonville.”

Within seconds, the system returns a list of relevant investigations and zooms the map to the Auckland suburb of Hobsonville. The displayed results include underground test information such as:

- Soil composition
- Water levels
- Rock layers

Ellis-Garland uses this kind of data to advise clients at geotechnical engineering firm ENGEO on what can be built above ground and how.

## NZGD and BEYON

The article attributes these AI-driven navigation and query capabilities to an upgraded New Zealand Geotechnical Database (NZGD) hosted on **BEYON**, a digital twin platform developed by global engineering consultancy **Beca**.

Beca describes BEYON as providing a:

- “High fidelity representation” of the real world
- Model that “looks like, behaves like and is connected to the real world”

(Quote attributed to Stephen Witherden, Beca’s product strategy manager.)

## Microsoft Azure and Azure OpenAI implementation

The article states:

- **NZGD** and **BEYON** both run on **Microsoft Azure**.
- BEYON’s AI assistant is powered by **OpenAI’s GPT-5.1** large language model.
- The assistant was developed using **Azure OpenAI** in **Microsoft Foundry** (Azure AI Foundry).

## Reported outcome

Ellis-Garland says the upgraded NZGD and AI tool have improved responsiveness by bringing up large amounts of information quickly:

> “The NZGD now and the AI tool has made us really responsive because it can just bring up so much information so quickly.”

She summarizes its practical value as:

> “This is our gateway to the ground.”
