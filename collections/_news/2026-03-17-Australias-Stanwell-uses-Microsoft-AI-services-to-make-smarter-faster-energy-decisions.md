In this news story, stclarke describes how Stanwell Corporation built the Stanwell Modelling Platform on Microsoft Azure and Microsoft AI services to support battery charge/discharge optimisation, electricity demand forecasting, and faster risk simulations across the business.<!--excerpt_end-->

## Overview

Stanwell Corporation, a government-owned energy utility in Queensland, describes building the **Stanwell Modelling Platform (SMP)**—an “AI brain in the cloud”—to support decision-making as its generation portfolio expands beyond coal into **wind, solar, and battery storage**.

According to Stanwell CIO **Kevin Lin**, SMP is used for “mission critical tasks like forecasting energy demand, supporting trading decisions, and managing safety risks,” reducing work that used to take weeks down to hours, minutes, or seconds.

## Platform foundation

- **Built on Microsoft Azure** and **Microsoft AI services**
- Started as a **machine learning** experiment in the trading department
- Expanded into an enterprise platform used across multiple teams
- Centralises Stanwell’s **data** and **predictive models** so engineers and analysts can reuse and operationalise them

Example questions teams use SMP to answer:

- *How much power will Queensland need next week?*
- *What’s the best way to run battery storage today?*
- *Which safety inspections should we prioritise this month?*

## Smarter energy trading

Stanwell’s electricity trading operates on a market that shifts every **five minutes**. SMP supports traders with AI-driven analysis of market data to recommend when to:

- Buy
- Sell
- Store energy

Stanwell Executive General Manager Business Services **Sophie Naughton** describes increased **weather-dependent generation** (including widespread solar) as a driver of market volatility and a growing “data tsunami,” making software support critical.

### Battery optimisation impact

A major example described is Stanwell’s **large-scale battery system**:

- An AI model optimises when the battery **charges** or **discharges**
- Stanwell reports **>200% improvement in asset performance**
- The company is exploring extending this optimisation across more generation and storage assets

Operationally, efficient battery dispatch can:

- Absorb excess solar generation during the day
- Release energy during evening demand peaks
- Help stabilise supply and reduce volatility

## Better demand forecasting

Stanwell also migrated forecasting models to the Azure-powered platform to use:

- Additional data sources (including more detailed **weather data** and usage patterns)
- More sophisticated algorithms

Reported outcome:

- **30% improvement in forecast accuracy** for certain time frames

Stated benefits include:

- More reliable planning for high-demand days
- Less overproduction (waste) and underproduction (spot-price spikes)
- Improved energy security and potentially more stable prices for consumers

## Faster scenario simulations for risk management

SMP is also used for “what-if” scenarios (e.g., market prices or fuel costs):

- Previously: simulations could take hours and require costly resources
- With Azure: runs at a fraction of the cost and, in some scenarios, **up to 15× faster**

This enables more frequent and broader scenario analysis to respond to volatility.

## Organisational adoption and governance

Stanwell positions SMP’s rollout as both a technology and culture shift:

- Early quick wins helped adoption beyond the original trading team
- Leadership backed scaling the platform company-wide
- IT and compliance were involved early to integrate with:
  - Data pipelines
n  - Reporting tools
  - User access controls
  - Security protocols
