# Microsoft Expands AI Skilling Initiatives to Reach 3 Million Saudis by 2030

Microsoft has announced a significant expansion of its AI skilling and education initiatives in Saudi Arabia. Building on the progress achieved in partnership with local authorities, the company aims to help three million people across the Kingdom acquire AI skills by 2030. This initiative aligns with Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030 workforce and technology priorities.

## Key Highlights

- **Microsoft Elevate for Educators**: Launch of a new program offering free AI literacy credentials and access to a global learning community for educators in Saudi Arabia. The initiative provides capacity building and practical resources to integrate AI into teaching and learning.

- **Targeted Outreach**: The company will support over 500,000 educators and roll out specific programs to expand access for women, ensuring inclusivity in all skilling efforts.

- **Broad National Partnerships**: Ongoing collaboration with the Ministry of Communications and Information Technology (MCIT), Ministry of Education, and organizations such as SDAIA and DGA. These efforts have already resulted in more than a million individuals participating in AI, cloud, and data courses, exceeding original targets.

- **Integration of Microsoft 365**: Widespread deployment across schools and universities, embedding secure digital and AI tools into daily education, and helping students, teachers, and administrators build practical digital skills.

- **AI Academy and Samai Initiative**: Microsoft, in collaboration with SDAIA, established the Microsoft AI Academy and contributed to the Samai Initiative, providing training to over a million Saudis and forming one of the largest national AI education efforts.

- **New Community Skilling Programs**: The new Microsoft Datacenter Academy will be the first of its kind in the Middle East, offering specialized training for community workforce and government employees. Additional programs have supported thousands of women, educators, and public sector leaders.

- **Executive Training**: The AI Global Leadership Program, delivered with MCIT, has trained decision-makers from the Saudi public and private sectors at Microsoft centers in Europe and the US.

## Government Support

Officials recognize Microsoft's role as a strategic partner in developing Saudi talent and opening opportunities, particularly for women and future technology professionals. These investments are expected to support innovation and economic growth.

## Conclusion

Microsoft's expanded AI skilling commitment underlines the company's focus on building a future-ready workforce in Saudi Arabia, leveraging partnerships, targeted capacity building, and practical integration of AI tools to equip millions for the digital economy.
