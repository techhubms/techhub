# The Economics of Enterprise AI: Insights from Forrester's Study on Microsoft Foundry

## Introduction

Customers are seeking smarter AI models, advanced agents, and robust solutions that operationalize AI workflows. Leaders are reframing their business systems as human-led and agent-operated environments. According to Forrester's Total Economic Impact™ study, organizations leveraging Microsoft Foundry, an Azure-based AI platform, saw significant increases in developer productivity, translating to a 327% ROI over three years and payback periods under six months.

## Productivity and Value Drivers

The Forrester study pinpointed developer productivity as the primary driver of ROI, attributing $15.7 million in value over three years to improvements in technical team efficiency. Common bottlenecks in AI initiatives, such as managing fragmented tools and bespoke governance processes, were minimized for Foundry customers, who reported up to 35% productivity gains.

### Operational Benefits

Surveyed organizations realized operational efficiencies, improved customer experience, and reduced technical debt by consolidating on the Foundry platform. In a composite enterprise scenario modeled by Forrester (with $10B in revenue, 25,000 employees, and 100 technical staff), the three-year present value of quantified benefits reached $49.5 million, outpacing the $11.6 million investment in resources.

## Standardization, Reusability, and Governance

Foundry enables teams to develop AI applications and agents on a unified platform, with features promoting reuse: standardized templates, reusable knowledge bases, evaluations, and governance controls. Customer feedback highlighted a 30–40% reduction in overall development time.

### Foundry IQ and Platform Thinking

75% of teams cited easier model grounding or knowledge integration with Foundry IQ. The platform's unified approach lowered infrastructure costs by $4.3M over three years compared to maintaining disparate container-based solutions for AI model development. Organizations transitioning from fragmented point solutions to a consolidated platform reported simpler, stronger execution and cost avoidance through decommissioning legacy workflows.

## AI Trust, Security, and Expandability

Security, privacy, and governance emerged as the most critical factors driving adoption of Microsoft Foundry, even above access to advanced models or lowering costs. Foundry Control Plane enables centralized governance, policy management, guardrails, and compliance observability across diverse environments. Controlled, auditable AI usage accelerates expansion from process automation to higher-impact, customer-facing applications.

## Strategic Recommendations

The study concludes that enterprise AI ROI is highest when organizations treat AI as a platform, not a collection of point solutions. Providing developers with repeatable, scalable workflows and tools accelerates project outcomes and builds organizational confidence in adopting AI at scale.
