# Establishing a Proactive Defense with Microsoft Security Exposure Management

**Author: Adi Shua Zucker**

A new guide from Microsoft Security introduces a practical framework for organizations seeking to move beyond fragmented security practices and toward an integrated, risk-driven exposure management strategy.

## Illuminating and Reducing Risk

Effective exposure management starts with gaining clear visibility across your entire attack surface and evolving toward hardening those risks systematically. Microsoft's e-book, [Establishing proactive defense—A maturity-based guide for adopting a dynamic, risk-based approach to exposure management](https://aka.ms/MSEMmaturity), provides clear steps for security teams at every stage to transform reactive tactics into a unified operating model focused on proactive risk reduction.

## The Five Levels of Exposure Management Maturity

The guide breaks down maturity into five actionable levels:

1. **Limited Visibility and Reactive Fixes**: Early-stage teams primarily react to security incidents with basic compliance measures.
2. **Consistent Processes**: Organizations begin standardizing approaches, but prioritization may lack business context.
3. **Prioritization with Business Context**: Security decisions begin to incorporate real business risks, not just isolated findings.
4. **Unified Risk Management**: Controls are validated, risk and asset data are consolidated, and mitigation is actively tested and improved.
5. **Continuous, Business-Aligned Defense**: The program evolves into a strategic discipline, with telemetry-driven, adaptive response and alignment to business objectives.

Teams can assess their current stage, identify pain points, and use the guide's recommendations for progressing toward a more advanced, resilient security posture.

![Graphic illustrating the levels of exposure management maturity.](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Picture1-1.jpg)

## Integrated, Risk-Driven Approach

Unified exposure management brings together assets, identities, cloud posture, and attack path analysis. This integration breaks down silos and enables a single, coherent view for security, IT, and risk stakeholders—facilitating faster, more consistent, and more strategic decision-making. Instead of isolated responses, the organization develops a systematic, repeatable security program.

## Ongoing Evolution, Not a Finish Line

The maturity model emphasizes that security is not a static goal. Even the most advanced programs must adapt to evolving threats using real-time telemetry and continuous validation. Strategic, business-aligned exposure management positions teams to allocate resources effectively and deliver measurable outcomes, rather than simply reacting to incidents.
