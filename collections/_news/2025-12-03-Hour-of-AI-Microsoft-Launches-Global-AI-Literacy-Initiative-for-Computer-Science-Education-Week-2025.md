# Hour of AI: Microsoft Launches Global AI Literacy Initiative for Computer Science Education Week 2025

Microsoft has launched the **Hour of AI** initiative, a global program for Computer Science Education Week (CSEdWeek) 2025, aimed at making AI learning accessible and engaging for all students and educators.

## Overview

Hour of AI is designed to:

- Provide free, age-appropriate resources and activities for classrooms and learners worldwide
- Spark curiosity and develop foundational skills in Artificial Intelligence
- Empower up to 25 million learners to explore, understand, and create with AI
- Support educators with training and ready-to-use lesson plans

Microsoft partners with organizations such as CSforALL and Code.org to reach students of all backgrounds and experience levels.

## Activities and Learning Resources

### 1. **Minecraft Hour of AI: The First Night**

- An activity where students survive their first night in Minecraft with help from AI agents
- Teaches algorithmic thinking and basic AI concepts through guided prompts and gameplay
- Includes free demo access, educator lesson plans, and is available in 29 languages
- [Access Minecraft Hour of AI resources](https://aka.ms/hourofai)
- [Facilitator training for educators](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/facilitate-minecraft-hour-ai-experience/)

### 2. **Bug Arena from MakeCode**

- Game-based tutorial where students design coded algorithms for bugs that then compete in an arena
- Focuses on coding, problem-solving, and understanding AI through interactive competition
- Suitable for grades 3–8
- [Get the Bug Arena lesson](https://aka.ms/bug-arena)

### 3. **Slither Slam from Visual Studio Code for Education**

- Game-based AI coding lesson aimed at high school learners (grades 9–12)
- Students use natural language prompts to generate code for a classic Snake game and compete against other AI-driven snakes
- Encourages experimentation with AI-generated code and prompt engineering
- [Access Slither Slam resources](https://aka.ms/slither-slam)

## Additional Programs

- **Microsoft Elevate**: Year-round resources and programs to help teachers bring AI into classrooms globally
- [AI Skills for Educators](https://aka.ms/AISkillsforEducators): Free training and resources on AI literacy
- **Microsoft Innovative Educator Expert (MIEE) Program** and **Showcase Schools Program**: Professional development and global collaboration opportunities for educators

## How to Participate

- Download lesson plans and educator guides for each Hour of AI activity
- Join the initiative during CS Ed Week (Dec. 8–14, 2025) or use resources year-round
- Bring AI experiences into classrooms, after-school programs, or at home
- [Learn more and get started with Hour of AI](https://aka.ms/hourofai)

## Visual Highlights

- Activities include interactive graphics, game-based learning, and collaborative student challenges.

Microsoft's Hour of AI initiative supports teachers, caregivers, and students in exploring the foundational concepts of AI, coding, and algorithmic thinking in a fun and inclusive environment.
