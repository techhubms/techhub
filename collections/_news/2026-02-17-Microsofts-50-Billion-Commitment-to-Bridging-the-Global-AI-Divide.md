# Microsoft's $50 Billion Commitment to Bridging the Global AI Divide

Microsoft announced at the India AI Impact Summit a major investment to help close the gap in artificial intelligence adoption between the Global North and South. By 2030, the company aims to invest $50 billion to make AI more accessible, inclusive, and impactful for countries that lag in AI diffusion.

## The AI Divide: Challenges and Opportunities

Microsoft’s latest AI Diffusion Report indicates a significant disparity in AI adoption and usage between the Global North and South, where usage in the North is about twice as high. This gap threatens to perpetuate economic inequality unless action is taken.

## Five-Part Plan to Drive AI Impact

Microsoft's strategy addresses multiple barriers to AI diffusion:

1. **Infrastructure Investments**: Expanding datacenter projects, connectivity initiatives, and electricity solutions to support AI readiness in regions like India, Africa, Latin America, and Southeast Asia. The company invested $8 billion in data infrastructure in these areas last year alone.

2. **Empowering Communities with Technology and Skills**: Through programs like Microsoft Elevate and Elevate for Educators, the goal is to provide AI education and cloud technology access to schools, teachers, and non-profits, aiming to equip millions of individuals and teachers with practical AI skills.

3. **Strengthening Multilingual and Multicultural AI Capabilities**: Microsoft is working on projects such as LINGUA Africa to improve AI understanding of underrepresented languages. This extends to developing and benchmarking new language models (as with Paza and Samiksha), and collaborating with organizations to ensure digital content provenance across linguistic boundaries.

4. **Enabling Local Innovation**: The company supports community-driven AI projects addressing tangible problems, such as food security in Africa (with NASA Harvest and local partners) and sustainable agriculture in India. Projects like Project Gecko focus on co-designing AI with local communities.

5. **Measuring AI Diffusion for Future Policy**: By refining methods to track and analyze AI adoption globally—including metrics from GitHub and Azure Foundry—Microsoft hopes to guide better policy and investment and accelerate responsible AI growth.

## Partnership and Trust

Success, according to Microsoft, will require deep, multi-sector partnerships and substantial foreign direct investment that respects digital sovereignty. Through alliances like the Trusted Tech Alliance, the company promotes secure, inclusive, and trustworthy deployment of technologies.

## Impact in Numbers

- $50 billion planned investment by 2030 in the Global South
- Over $8 billion invested in data infrastructure in the last fiscal year
- 117 million people in Africa reached with improved connectivity
- Ambitious targets to provide AI skills and credentials to 20 million people by 2028, with an additional 20 million in India by 2030

## Conclusion

Microsoft’s comprehensive approach combines infrastructure, education, research, and data to foster equitable AI growth. With ongoing initiatives and partnerships, the company’s efforts aim to ensure AI becomes a driver for opportunity and prosperity globally.
