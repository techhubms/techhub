# Introducing Maia 200: The AI Accelerator Built for Inference

Microsoft has unveiled the Maia 200, a custom AI accelerator optimized specifically for inference workloads in the Azure cloud. This marks a significant investment in purpose-built silicon and systems designed to meet the increasing demand for high-performance AI.

- [Deep dive into the Maia 200 architecture](http://aka.ms/Maia200arch): An in-depth technical analysis of the Maia 200's design and architecture, covering inner workings and optimization strategies.
- [AI chips are getting hotter. A microfluidics breakthrough goes straight to the silicon to cool up to three times better.](https://news.microsoft.com/source/features/innovation/microfluidics-liquid-cooling-ai-chips/): Explores Microsoft's adoption of microfluidics-based cooling solutions for their AI hardware.
- [Announcing Cobalt 200: Azure’s next cloud-native CPU](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/blog/azureinfrastructureblog/announcing-cobalt-200-azure-next-cloud-native-cpu/4469807): Details on Cobalt 200, another custom chip designed for Azure, showing Microsoft's broader silicon strategy.
- [Azure Maia for the era of AI: From silicon to software to systems](https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/blog/azure-maia-for-the-era-of-ai-from-silicon-to-software-to-systems/): Discusses the systems-level approach that integrates Maia hardware with Azure's software and services.
- [With a systems approach to chips, Microsoft aims to tailor everything ‘from silicon to service’ to meet AI demand](https://news.microsoft.com/source/features/ai/in-house-chips-silicon-to-service-to-meet-ai-demand/): Outlines how Microsoft is engineering every layer, from silicon to full stack services, for AI scalability and performance.

## Overview

- **Maia 200** is engineered to accelerate inference for large-scale AI models running in Azure.
- Microsoft is pairing hardware innovations with advanced cooling, such as microfluidics, to address operational challenges at scale.
- The Maia 200 fits within a broader ecosystem of custom silicon, including the new Cobalt 200 CPU, for optimized cloud-native and AI workloads.
- This holistic approach seeks to optimize Azure’s infrastructure for demanding enterprise AI applications, both in terms of compute power and energy efficiency.
