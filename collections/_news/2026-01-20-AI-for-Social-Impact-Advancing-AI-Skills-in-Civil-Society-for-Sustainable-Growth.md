# AI for Social Impact: Advancing AI Skills in Civil Society for Sustainable Growth

Microsoft Thailand joined forces with the United Nations Economic and Social Commission for Asia and the Pacific (ESCAP), CCDKM at Sukhothai Thammathirat Open University, and the Digital Economy Promotion Agency (depa) to launch the 'AI for Social Impact' training program. Running from November 2025 to March 2026, this initiative aims to enhance digital and generative AI skills for personnel and leaders of civil society organizations in Thailand.

The program draws on the vision of the Microsoft Elevate project, aiming to share AI knowledge, promote digital inclusion, and reduce skill gaps in Thai society.

## Program Highlights

- **Developed by Microsoft Thailand**: Curriculum focuses on foundational Generative AI concepts and practical, hands-on usage.
- **Microsoft Copilot**: Participants learn to use Copilot for content creation, data analysis, and improving work efficiency in the social sector.
- **Foundational and Advanced Training**: Covers systematic prompt engineering, use of AI for social media, campaign design, data management, and analysis.
- **Delivery and Access**: Training takes place on-site and online, with additional self-paced learning available via smartmooc.org and the Microsoft AI Skills Navigator.

## Partner Contributions

- **ESCAP**: Provides regional context and helps raise digital standards throughout Asia-Pacific.
- **CCDKM and STOU**: Link academic knowledge and act as hubs for social sector skill development.
- **depa**: Focuses on national digital literacy policy and capacity-building.

## Statements from Leaders

- **Supahrat Juramongkol (Microsoft Thailand)**: Stressed the importance of responsible and practical AI skills training to empower communities and improve organizational efficiency, communication, and data analysis.
- **Ruhimat Soerakoesoemah (ESCAP)**: Emphasized that the initiative drives progress towards the Sustainable Development Goals and ensures technology benefits all people and communities.
- **Associate Prof. Dr. Taweewat Wattanakuljaroen (STOU)**: Highlighted the role of AI in building the skills and potential needed for Thailand’s long-term strength.
- **Dr. Sithon Kulradathon (depa)**: Reaffirmed the mission to equip citizens with digital intelligence in parallel with infrastructure development.

## How to Participate

- Explore 200+ Thai-language AI courses online at [Microsoft AI Skills Navigator](https://aiskillsnavigator.microsoft.com/th-th)
- Self-paced courses available at [smartmooc.org](https://smartmooc.org/courses?search_query=ai)
- Stay updated on events via the [AI for Social Impact Facebook page](https://www.facebook.com/AIforSocialImpact)

The 'AI for Social Impact' initiative represents a significant, collaborative step towards embedding AI capability and awareness across Thailand’s civil society organizations.
