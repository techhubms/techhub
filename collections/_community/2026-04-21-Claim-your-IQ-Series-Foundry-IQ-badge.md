# Claim your IQ Series: Foundry IQ badge

The IQ Series is introducing community badges to recognize developers building on the Microsoft IQ stack. **Foundry IQ** is the first badge.

To earn it, you complete **all three Foundry IQ episode cookbooks**, then submit proof and request the badge.

## What the badge represents

The **IQ Series: Foundry IQ** badge recognizes developers who completed the Foundry IQ curriculum end-to-end:

- Deployed **Azure AI Search**, **Azure OpenAI**, a **Foundry project**, and **Azure Blob Storage** with seeded sample data
- Connected structured and unstructured sources into Foundry IQ
- Built and queried multi-source AI knowledge bases
- Grounded agent responses in permission-aware enterprise knowledge

Badges are issued by the [Global AI Community](https://globalai.community/). You’ll need an account there before submitting.

## What the three episodes cover

### Episode 1 — Unlocking Knowledge for Your Agents
Introduces Foundry IQ and the core ideas behind it:

- How AI agents work with knowledge
- The main Foundry IQ components that support knowledge-driven applications

### Episode 2 — Building the Data Pipeline with Knowledge Sources
Focuses on **Knowledge Sources** and how content flows into Foundry IQ across:

- SharePoint
- Fabric
- OneLake
- Azure Blob Storage
- Azure AI Search
- The web

### Episode 3 — Querying the Multi-Source AI Knowledge Bases
Dives into **Knowledge Bases** and how multiple sources can be organized behind a single endpoint:

- Querying across sources
- Synthesizing information to answer complex questions

Each episode is paired with a hands-on cookbook, and all three reuse the same Azure deployment (set up once, build across all three).

## How to claim the badge

Follow these steps in order:

1. Fork the [IQ Series repo](https://github.com/microsoft/iq-series) and complete all three episode cookbooks in your fork.
   - Commit your notebooks with cell outputs saved (this is the proof of completion).
2. Capture a final output screenshot for each episode.
   - Your GitHub username or Azure resource name must be visible.
3. Submit a badge request issue using the template:
   - [foundry-iq-badge-request.yml](https://github.com/microsoft/iq-series/issues/new?template=foundry-iq-badge-request.yml)
   - The template asks for fork URLs, screenshots, and one brief technical takeaway per episode.
4. Complete the badge form:
   - [https://aka.ms/iq-series/badge-form](https://aka.ms/iq-series/badge-form)
   - This is required to issue the badge.

## Why this badge is worth your time

The IQ Series badge is meant to reflect hands-on learning with real infrastructure:

- Real indexed data
- Real agents and queries
- Real end-to-end platform work (grounding, retrieval, knowledge-aware agents)

The post also notes that **Work IQ** and **Fabric IQ** are coming next, each with its own badge.
