Microsoft Developer shares Patrick Chanezon’s DevX Summit 2026 talk on how AI coding agents (including GitHub Copilot CLI) are changing day-to-day developer workflows, pushing engineers toward higher-level work and new habits for managing “teams of agents” while dealing with AI fatigue and known imperfections.<!--excerpt_end-->

## Overview

This DevX Summit (March 2026) session from Patrick Chanezon (VP of Developer Relations, Microsoft) discusses how **AI coding agents** have progressed over the prior 6 months and what that means for software developers.

Key themes include:

- Developers shifting to a **higher level of abstraction**
- Learning to **manage teams of agents** (rather than doing every task manually)
- Impacts on **early-career developers**
- How to **hire and train** for an agent-heavy workflow
- Developing “hygiene” and work habits to deal with **AI fatigue**
- Staying aware of **imperfections and limits** in AI systems

## What’s demonstrated / discussed

### GitHub Copilot CLI in practice

The talk includes a demo segment on using **GitHub Copilot CLI** to assist with tasks such as making presentations and working with images.

### Working with multiple agents

The session touches on the idea of **sub-agents** and developers acting more like **managers of agents**, coordinating work and validating outputs.

### Skills and workflow changes

Topics covered include:

- New skills developers may need as coding agents become more capable
- Redefining parts of the software engineering profession
- Practical work habits to reduce burnout when working with fast, always-on systems
